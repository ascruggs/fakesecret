mail in their secrets anonymously on one side of a postcard.

Michelle, Ruthie, Andrew and Tyler are the current PostSecret Interns helping sort and archive more than 500,000 secrets. They asked me to pass along a few reminders.

1. You can mail your new secrets to;
PostSecret, 13345 Copper Ridge Road, Germantown, MD 20874

2. The new URL for the PostSecret Blog is, http://www.postsecret.com

3. Postcards 6" x 4" are ideal for archiving.

4. Postcards don't have spell-check.

5. Please no glitter or blood.





Interns also help moderate the different discussion forums in the PostSecret Community and assist with special projects like art exhibits.





For more information about PostSecret internships, live events and art exhibits join here on Facebook or use the Facebook link at the bottom of this page.

Nicole, a member of the first PostSecret intern team, shares her story below.





I was one of the first PostSecret interns, starting in March 2007 after hearing Frank speak at a local event.  I thought he was joking when he offhandedly mentioned needing interns, but decided I would approach him about it anyways, despite the long line afterward at the book-signing.

I have been the administrator of the PostSecret Community (PSC) since its inception.  My favorite part has been planning and organizing the PSC picnic that is now an annual event.  The idea was for members of the online community to meet the persons in real life with whom they are sharing their secrets.  The past two years have been a huge success, and I have loved getting to know members of the PSC on a personal level, bringing a new depth to my work with the project.

PostSecret's dedication to the Hopeline inspired me to start working the crisis lines.  I went through the training and once a week was answering calls for 1-800-SUICIDE.  It was daunting at first, but far more rewarding and challenging than any other volunteer experience I have been a part of.  When Frank posted 1-800-SUICIDE's call for volunteers for the new upcoming online crisis center, I signed up, and knowing I'd be graduating college in a few short months, asked Frank if Reese, the president of 1-800-SUICIDE had any job opportunities.

Reese and I got in touch and I helped bring the first ever Alive! Mental Health Fair to my school's campus and also helped plan the 10th anniversary Reason To Live concert featuring Blue October.  It was a great experience for both Reese and myself, and upon graduation, which was just a few short weeks ago, I was offered a position with 1-800-SUICIDE.  I am now working for an organization I love and in a job that is perfect for me.  My main responsibilities include managing the social networking, fundraising, and organizing the intern program.  I am really looking forward to the future.  1-800-SUICIDE has several new programs that are about to be launched, and it is an exciting time to be working with the Hopeline.

I continue to manage the PostSecret online forum and help in other ways. It has been an amazing past three years.  My work with PostSecret has not only given me many opportunities but also helped me grow as a person to become less judgmental and more open and empathetic.  I am grateful for my continued opportunities with PostSecret and looking forward to seeing all that working with the Hopeline brings.
